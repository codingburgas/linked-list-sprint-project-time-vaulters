Name: The Moon Landing
Date: July 20th 1969

On July 20, 1969, during the Apollo 11 mission, NASA astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to land on the Moon. Armstrong's iconic words, "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind," marked the moment of landing. The mission aimed to demonstrate American superiority in space exploration during the Cold War-era Space Race. The astronauts conducted experiments, collected lunar samples, and spent approximately 21 hours on the lunar surface before returning to Earth.
